Introduction

There are differing beliefs about the existence of so-called “ki”—a force said to influence people in various ways. Some individuals are firmly convinced of its reality, while others remain highly skeptical. Moreover, it seems that many people show little interest in ki altogether.

Nevertheless, it is also a fact that there are numerous reports of people recovering their health or even curing illnesses through the use of ki. In addition, martial arts such as Aikido, where ki plays a central role, as well as Qigong and healing with hands (commonly referred to as “Teate therapy”), have continued to be passed down and maintained in society rather than disappearing.

The concept of ki has existed in China since ancient times and has been transmitted across the ages up to the present day. This longevity suggests that ki contains some form of truth or value for human beings. In this website, I will focus on healing with hands—known as Teate therapy.

Teate therapy, which is closely related to the primal experience of a parent expressing love and care for a child, is sometimes explained psychologically or medically as the effect of oxytocin (commonly known as the “happiness hormone”), as noted by Dr. Hajime Yamaguchi of J. F. Oberlin University. However, in this discussion, we will regard such effects as part of the broader workings of ki.

Although the author’s field of expertise is engineering and not ki, I will briefly share personal experiences, impressions, and knowledge regarding the nature, effects, and healing properties of ki.
